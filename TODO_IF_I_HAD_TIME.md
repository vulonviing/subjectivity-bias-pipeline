# To-Do — If I Had Time

Out-of-scope ideas captured during the 4–6h build window. Not part of the delivered pipeline.

- **Topic modeling (BERTopic)** — Zamanım olsaydı mevcut URL/feed keyword kuralı yerine corpus üzerinde BERTopic çalıştırıp veri-güdümlü kümelerden topic ataması yapardım. Keyword kuralı basit ve hızlı ama editorial section adlarına bağımlı; BERTopic metin içeriğine bakarak daha sağlıklı, outlet-agnostic topic etiketleri üretir. Ayrıca generic "News" feed'inden gelen ve section sinyali olmadığı için `uncategorized` kalan makaleler için de otomatik atama yapılabilirdi.
- Balance the **non-political** bucket across blocks — it is currently underrepresented in the left block (left=9 vs right=33). Would add non-political left-leaning feeds (e.g. Guardian `/sport/`, `/culture/`, `/lifeandstyle/`, HuffPost entertainment vertical) so the corpus supports political-vs-non-political comparisons within each block.
- **Branded columnist articles (Fox / CyberGuy)** — Fox News carries Kurt "the CyberGuy" tech columns (and possibly other branded properties: Outkick, Tucker, etc.) where the entire article is a promotional column with brand identity baked into editorial voice. Sample `255f62b3af249a9a04e4e48f196d77e6b9145236` has 5 CTA lines, multiple brand mentions, and a newsletter signup with near-zero news content. A future pass would either (a) detect via brand-mention density and drop such articles from the corpus, or (b) build a per-outlet "branded column" list to mask, requiring manual curation.
- **NPR show names and correspondent names as outlet signals** — NPR's identity leaks through branded show names ("Up First", "Morning Edition", "All Things Considered", "Newsmakers", "Fresh Air") and named correspondents (e.g. Greg Myre, Leila Fadel) cited in articles. Adding these as simple aliases risks false positives (show-title words appear in general titles; surnames appear as quoted sources). A future pass would maintain a curated NPR-specific entity list combined with line-context heuristics (e.g. "`[OUTLET]'s <Firstname Lastname> tells <ShowName>`" pattern).
- **Replace NPR with a written-news outlet** — NPR RSS beslemeleri yazılı haber yerine radyo transcript'i (HOST:/BYLINE:/SOUNDBITE formatı) döndürdüğünden dataset'i kirletti ve cleaning aşamasında ciddi iş yükü yarattı. Bir sonraki iterasyonda NPR yerine AP News, Reuters, Politico veya The Hill gibi yalnızca yazılı haber üreten bir outlet tercih edilmeli.
- **Cross-outlet references intentionally kept** — Mentions of Washington Post, NYT, CNN, MSNBC, WSJ, ABC/CBS/NBC News are deliberately **not** masked. When an outlet criticizes or references another outlet by name (e.g. Fox coverage of CNN, Guardian citing NYT), this is itself a bias signal: it reflects the editorial stance of the writing outlet. Masking it would suppress the very signal the pipeline is trying to surface for annotation. This is a design choice, not an oversight.
